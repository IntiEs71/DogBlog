Summertime Safety for Dogs

By: Inti Espejo
May 20th, 2022
LinkedIn

As stated by the American Kennel Club,

hot weather can also make us uncomfortable, and it poses special risks for dogs

So we begin to embark on Summer days, we'll once again start to spend more time outside and take our necessary steps to stay safe in the sun. But we must not forget about making sure our pets are also safe and cool during warm days. Here are some tips to keep your dog safe from the Summer heat.

  • Walk at Non-Peak Hours
    If your dog enjoys their daily walks but it's too hot outside. Try seeing if you can walk during non-peak sunny hours, such as early before sunrise or after sunset. Before going out make sure the surfaces outside such as the asphalt and sidewalk are not too hot.
  • Carry Plenty of Water
    When you do decide to take your dog out, it is important to monitor your dog for any unusual behavior and to check for signs of heat fatigue and heat stroke. It's important to keep your dog well hydrated to avoid fatigue, so be sure to carry plenty of water.
In order to maintain our dogs' safety it's important to follow these tips and use your best judgement on deciding when it's too hot to go outside or whether we need to take additional precautionary measures.
